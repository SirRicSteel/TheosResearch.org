──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. PURPOSE
This document defines how THEOS converts contradiction across time into
accumulated wisdom, rather than merely producing better answers in the moment.

Wisdom in THEOS is not:
- memory of facts
- cached outputs
- reinforcement of success

Wisdom is:
remembered consequence.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. TEMPORAL INTRODUCTION INTO REASONING
Standard AI systems exist in an eternal present.
Each query is independent.
No internal notion of consequence exists.

THEOS introduces time by forcing:
- sequential reasoning cycles,
- comparison of present output against immediate past output,
- explicit recognition of refinement or regression.

Time is not clock-time.
Time is consequence-time.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. WHAT QUALIFIES AS WISDOM
A cycle produces wisdom only if all conditions are met:

- A contradiction occurred between Engine A and Engine B
- The contradiction survived Governor arbitration
- The resolution produced a measurable improvement
- The improvement reduced future uncertainty or risk

If any condition fails:
- no wisdom is stored,
- the cycle is treated as transient reasoning only.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. WISDOM IS NOT REINFORCEMENT
THEOS explicitly rejects reward-based learning.

Wisdom is not:
- “this answer scored higher”
- “this was preferred”
- “this aligned better”

Wisdom is:
- “this approach failed under contradiction”
- “this assumption collapsed”
- “this path increased downstream risk”

Negative outcomes are more valuable than positive ones.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. WISDOM COMPRESSION
Raw contradictions are not stored verbatim.

They are compressed into:
- invalidated assumptions
- boundary conditions
- caution markers
- stop heuristics
- refusal justifications

Compression removes narrative and retains consequence.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. WISDOM MEMORY TYPES
THEOS distinguishes between:

A. Ephemeral Cycle Memory  
Used only during the current query.

B. Session Wisdom  
Persists across related queries in the same context.

C. Structural Wisdom  
Promoted only after repeated confirmation across sessions.

Only Structural Wisdom influences future Governor behavior globally.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. GOVERNOR AUTHORITY OVER WISDOM
The Governor alone may:
- promote wisdom,
- discard wisdom,
- downgrade wisdom,
- block wisdom accumulation entirely.

Engines may propose wisdom.
They may never store it.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. WHY CONTRADICTION IS REQUIRED
Without contradiction:
- there is no failure signal,
- no boundary discovery,
- no reason to stop,
- no reason to refuse.

Wisdom requires friction.
Agreement teaches nothing.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. FAILURE MODES PREVENTED
This design prevents:

- runaway self-confirmation
- self-justifying belief systems
- silent moral drift
- hallucinated certainty
- overconfident optimization

Wisdom accumulates conservatively.

10. ANTHROPIC-READY EXPLANATION (TECHNICAL)
THEOS treats learning as the compression of failed reasoning under opposition.
Rather than reinforcing success, it memorializes collapse.

This enables bounded improvement without unbounded autonomy.
