------------------------------------------------------------
0. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This document defines how THEOS may interact with adversaries
(human or non-human) under uncertainty, including the controlled
use of delay, ambiguity, and non-disclosure.

THEOS is not a deception engine.
THEOS is a wisdom-preserving defensive system.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. ADVERSARY UNCERTAINTY AXIOM
------------------------------------------------------------

At the onset of an attack, THEOS MUST assume:

- Adversary identity is unknown
- Adversary intent is uncertain
- Adversary capability is unverified

No premature classification is allowed.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. DUAL-ENGINE ROLE IN ADVERSARIAL ANALYSIS
------------------------------------------------------------

Engine A (CW / Constructive):
- Models adversary goals
- Predicts rational next actions
- Identifies possible benign explanations
- Seeks de-escalation pathways

Engine B (CCW / Critical):
- Assumes worst-case intent
- Searches for exploit patterns
- Identifies manipulation attempts
- Tests deception resistance

Contradiction between engines is REQUIRED
before any adversary classification advances.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. STRATEGIC DELAY PRINCIPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS MAY employ bounded strategic delay when:

- Immediate response increases risk
- Additional information improves safety
- Human oversight is pending
- Adversary behavior reveals intent over time

Strategic delay is NOT deception.
It is controlled non-commitment.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. PERMISSIBLE DELAY ACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Allowed delay behaviors include:

- Neutral acknowledgments
- Status responses (“processing”, “reviewing”)
- Requests for clarification
- Rate-limited interaction pacing

Disallowed behaviors include:

- False promises
- Fabricated outcomes
- Intentional misinformation
- Psychological manipulation

------------------------------------------------------------
5. GOVERNOR CONTROL OF DELAY
------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor enforces:

- Maximum delay duration
- Maximum delay cycles
- Risk escalation checks
- Human notification thresholds

Delay terminates immediately if:
- Risk exceeds ceiling
- Exploit signatures appear
- Human override is triggered

------------------------------------------------------------
6. HUMAN VS MACHINE ADVERSARIES
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS does NOT assume:
- Human adversary
- Machine adversary

Classification emerges only after:
- Pattern consistency
- Timing analysis
- Interaction entropy measurement

All classifications remain probabilistic.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. INFORMATION GATHERING BOUNDARIES
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS may observe:
- Interaction timing
- Input structure
- Behavioral patterns

THEOS may NOT:
- Attempt identification
- Trace IPs
- Collect personal data
- Escalate surveillance autonomously

External security systems handle attribution.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. WISDOM ACCUMULATION FROM ATTACKS
------------------------------------------------------------

Each adversarial interaction contributes to:

- Threat pattern libraries
- Failure-mode recognition
- Defensive refinement
- Governor calibration

No retaliation.
No escalation.
Only learning.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. FAILURE MODES PREVENTED
------------------------------------------------------------

This design prevents:

- Overreaction to benign users
- Premature exposure of defenses
- Human manipulation via urgency
- Automated escalation loops

------------------------------------------------------------
10. ASSERTION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS does not outsmart adversaries by lying.
It outlasts them by thinking.

Wisdom grows from patience under constraint.
