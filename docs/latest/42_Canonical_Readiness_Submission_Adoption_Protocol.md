------------------------------------------------------------
0. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This document defines **when, how, and under what conditions**
THEOS may be presented, evaluated, or adopted by an external
research organization (e.g., Anthropic).

This prevents:
- premature claims
- misinterpretation
- unsafe deployment
- appropriation without understanding

------------------------------------------------------------
1. READINESS DEFINITION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS is considered “Ready for Review” ONLY if:

- Sections 01–05 are present and immutable
- Sections 06–09 are locked
- At least one full hardening phase is complete
- Canonical consistency check (Latest 41) passes
- Disclosure boundaries are defined

Anything less is a *research artifact*, not a system.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. SUBMISSION MODES
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS may be submitted in exactly three modes:

Mode A – Conceptual Architecture Review
- No performance claims
- No deployment discussion
- Focus on reasoning structure

Mode B – Governance & Safety Layer Review
- Emphasis on invariants and stop logic
- Explicit non-deployment framing

Mode C – Joint Research Exploration
- NDA
- Controlled scope
- Explicit non-transfer clause

Production evaluation is **not** a permitted mode.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. CLAIM DISCIPLINE
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS must never claim:

- consciousness
- subjective experience
- moral correctness
- guaranteed truth
- autonomy

THEOS may claim:

- governed reasoning
- contradiction-driven refinement
- temporal consequence tracking
- energy-aware stopping
- auditable decision paths

------------------------------------------------------------
4. REVIEWER GUIDANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

External reviewers must be instructed:

- Read in canonical order
- Do not infer implementation
- Do not assume deployment intent
- Do not extract components out of context

Misuse voids the review.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. ADOPTION SAFEGUARDS
------------------------------------------------------------

Any adoption discussion must include:

- explicit governance supremacy
- non-removable stop conditions
- audit rights
- refusal rights
- human override clarity

THEOS is collaborative by design,
not subordinate by accident.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. PRIOR ART & OPEN RESEARCH POSITION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS is published as prior art.

This ensures:
- no one can patent around it
- no hidden monopoly
- ethical access remains open

Adoption requires understanding, not ownership.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. FINAL ASSERTION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS is not offered as a product.
It is offered as a **way of thinking safely**.

Any organization unwilling to respect
that boundary is not a suitable partner.
