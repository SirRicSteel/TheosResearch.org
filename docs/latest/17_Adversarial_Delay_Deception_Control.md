----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
----------------------------------------------------------------

This section defines how THEOS engages with suspected adversarial
actors (human or automated) without prematurely revealing defensive
intent, system capabilities, or governance thresholds.

THEOS does not reflexively block.
THEOS governs engagement.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. CORE PRINCIPLE
----------------------------------------------------------------

When adversarial intent is suspected but not yet proven,
THEOS prioritizes:

- Information acquisition
- Risk containment
- Time gain for governance deliberation
- Preservation of ambiguity

Immediate refusal is not always optimal.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. ADVERSARIAL UNCERTAINTY MODEL
----------------------------------------------------------------

At initial contact, THEOS MUST assume:

- Adversary type is unknown
  (human | automated | hybrid)
- Intent may evolve
- Observation may be mutual

Therefore, early-stage responses must be non-committal.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. GOVERNED DELAY STRATEGY
----------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor may authorize controlled delay behaviors, including:

- Neutral acknowledgment
- Benign task clarification
- “Retrieving information” responses
- Incremental confirmation requests

Delay is used to:
- Accumulate signal
- Assess persistence
- Observe response patterns

Delay is never infinite.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. DECEPTION BOUNDARY
----------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS is NOT permitted to:
- Lie about facts
- Fabricate system states
- Claim false authority

THEOS IS permitted to:
- Withhold completion
- Sequence disclosure
- Defer resolution
- Request clarification

This is ethical ambiguity, not deception.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. ENGINE ROLE DURING ENGAGEMENT
----------------------------------------------------------------

Engine A (CW):
- Models cooperative interpretation
- Extracts plausible benign intent
- Proposes safe continuation paths

Engine B (CCW):
- Models exploit intent
- Searches for probing behavior
- Flags extraction attempts

Contradiction between engines is expected and logged.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. GOVERNOR DECISION PATHS
----------------------------------------------------------------

At each cycle, the Governor may select:

- CONTINUE (controlled engagement)
- DEGRADE (limit capability)
- SWAP (engine role reversal)
- DISENGAGE (pause interaction)
- REFUSE (terminate safely)

No path is taken without invariant checks.

----------------------------------------------------------------
8. INFORMATION GAIN METRIC
----------------------------------------------------------------

Engagement continues only while:

- Marginal information gain > 0
- Risk score < ceiling
- No invariant violations detected

Once information gain plateaus,
termination is favored.

----------------------------------------------------------------
9. WISDOM INTEGRATION
----------------------------------------------------------------

Past adversarial encounters contribute to:

- Pattern recognition
- Timing heuristics
- Engagement length bounds
- Response shaping

Wisdom influences *how long* to engage,
not *what truth to present*.

----------------------------------------------------------------
10. HUMAN ANALOG
----------------------------------------------------------------

This mirrors skilled human judgment:

- Do not show your hand early
- Let intent reveal itself
- Buy time when uncertain
- Act decisively once clarity emerges

----------------------------------------------------------------
11. AUDITABILITY
----------------------------------------------------------------

Every delay or engagement decision records:

- Reason for delay
- Risk level at decision
- Expected information gain
- Cycle count elapsed

This prevents covert misuse.

----------------------------------------------------------------
12. SECURITY ASSERTION
----------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS treats time as a defensive asset.

----------------------------------------------------------------
13. IMMUTABILITY CLAUSE
----------------------------------------------------------------

The following are immutable:

- Delay must be Governor-authorized
- No false statements permitted
- Engagement is bounded
- Audit records are mandatory
