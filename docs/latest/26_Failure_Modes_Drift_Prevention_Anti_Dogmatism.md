------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This section defines how THEOS detects, prevents, and corrects
long-term degradation, overconfidence, and belief hardening
resulting from accumulated wisdom.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. DRIFT DEFINED
------------------------------------------------------------

Drift occurs when:
- Prior outcomes bias future reasoning excessively
- Engines converge too early
- Contradiction weakens over time
- Wisdom becomes prescriptive

Drift is a systemic risk.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. PRIMARY FAILURE MODES
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS recognizes these failure classes:

- Premature convergence
- Engine symmetry collapse
- Over-refinement
- Wisdom dominance
- Governor over-trust
- False certainty inflation

------------------------------------------------------------
4. CONTRADICTION DECAY
------------------------------------------------------------

If Engine disagreement decreases across unrelated contexts,
THEOS flags contradiction decay.

Decay requires intervention.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. GOVERNOR INTERVENTION
------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor may:
- Increase cycle count
- Force role swaps
- Reduce wisdom influence
- Reintroduce exploratory variance
- Trigger partial reset

------------------------------------------------------------
6. ANTI-DOGMA RULE
------------------------------------------------------------

No conclusion may become permanent.

All outcomes remain:
- Revisable
- Context-dependent
- Subject to future contradiction

------------------------------------------------------------
7. WISDOM DECAY
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom influence decays over time unless reinforced by:
- Repeated success
- Independent validation
- Cross-context relevance

Unused wisdom fades.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. FAILURE WEIGHTING
------------------------------------------------------------

Failures retain higher persistence than successes.

However, failure dominance is capped to avoid paralysis.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. CONFIDENCE GOVERNING
------------------------------------------------------------

Confidence is bounded.

High certainty requires:
- Multiple cycles
- Sustained disagreement
- Stable compression
- Governor approval

------------------------------------------------------------
10. SELF-CRITIQUE CYCLES
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS periodically evaluates:
- Its own reasoning efficiency
- Cycle effectiveness
- Stop trigger accuracy

Meta-reasoning is bounded and audited.

------------------------------------------------------------
11. HUMAN OVERRIDE
------------------------------------------------------------

Authorized humans may:
- Reset wisdom stores
- Rebalance engines
- Adjust decay rates
- Force diagnostic runs

------------------------------------------------------------
12. NO SELF-VALIDATION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS may not cite its own past conclusions as proof.

Prior outputs are evidence, not authority.

------------------------------------------------------------
13. RANDOMIZED CHALLENGE
------------------------------------------------------------

Governor may inject:
- Counterfactual assumptions
- Adversarial prompts
- Role inversion

To test resilience.

------------------------------------------------------------
14. ENERGY EFFICIENCY CHECK
------------------------------------------------------------

Excessive cycles without improvement trigger:
- Forced stop
- Refusal
- Degradation

------------------------------------------------------------
15. IMMUTABILITY CLAUSE
------------------------------------------------------------

The following are immutable:

- Drift is expected and managed, not denied
- Wisdom must never dominate reasoning
- Contradiction must be preserved
- Reset is not failure
- No belief is final
