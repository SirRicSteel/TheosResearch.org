## 2. High-Level Architecture Overview

THEOS consists of three irreducible components:

1. **Engine A (Left / Clockwise / Constructive)**
2. **Engine B (Right / Counter-Clockwise / Adversarial)**
3. **Governor (Selector / Regulator / Arbiter)**

The two engines operate *simultaneously* and *in opposition*, like meshing gears.
The Governor sits above them, controlling engagement, cycle count, and termination.

---

## 3. The Triadic Reasoning Cycle (Single Engine)

Each engine reasons using a **triadic cycle**:

1. **Inductive Reasoning** – Pattern formation from observations  
2. **Abductive Reasoning** – Hypothesis generation to explain patterns  
3. **Deductive Reasoning** – Logical consequences derived from hypotheses  

### Clock Model
These phases are modeled as positions on a clock:

- **12 o’clock:** Inductive
- **4 o’clock:** Abductive
- **8 o’clock:** Deductive

A full rotation constitutes **one reasoning cycle**.

---

## 4. Recursive Cycles and the Introduction of Time

After completing one cycle:
- The **deductive output** is fed back into the next cycle’s **inductive phase**

This creates:
- A *momentary past*
- A record of **consequence**
- The ability for the engine to “think about what it just thought”

Multiple cycles introduce refinement, nuance, and temporal depth.

Typically:
- 1–2 cycles → practical answers
- 3+ cycles → theoretical or overfit detail

The Governor determines when to stop.

---

## 5. Dual Engines and Forced Contradiction

### Engine A — Clockwise (CW)
- Cycle order: **Inductive → Abductive → Deductive**
- Role: Constructive synthesis
- Bias: Building candidate solutions

### Engine B — Counter-Clockwise (CCW)
- Cycle order: **Inductive → Deductive → Abductive**
- Role: Critical pressure and destabilization
- Bias: Challenging assumptions and outcomes

They rotate **simultaneously** in opposite directions.

---

## 6. Gear Meshing and Contradictory Torque

The engines are modeled as **meshing gears** or **compression rollers**:

- Their opposing rotations create **friction**
- Friction produces **contradiction**
- Contradiction is **mandatory**, not optional

This is not randomness.
This is *mechanically enforced disagreement*.

Like:
- Steel rollers flattening raw metal
- Washing machine rollers extracting water
- Opposing cognitive pressures refining truth

---

## 7. Compression Produces Signal

Contradiction serves three purposes:
1. Exposes hidden assumptions
2. Eliminates fragile hypotheses
3. Increases signal-to-noise ratio

Only ideas that survive compression pass upward.

This is how **refinement occurs with less compute**, not more.

## 8. The Governor’s Role (Preview Only)

The Governor does **not** reason.

It:
- Selects which engines engage
- Sets cycle counts
- Adjusts compression pressure
- Allows clutching (temporary disengagement)
- Terminates when marginal value drops

The Governor governs *process*, not content.

## 9. Wisdom Emergence (Preview Only)

Wisdom is not memory.
Wisdom is **remembered consequence**.

Each failed or successful cycle leaves residue:

- Under what conditions

These residues inform future governance decisions.

No wisdom exists without:
- Recursive cycles
- Contradiction
- Time
