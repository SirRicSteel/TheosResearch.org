------------------------------------------------------------
0. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This document defines how humans and THEOS jointly govern
decisions, reasoning boundaries, escalation, and responsibility.

THEOS is not autonomous governance.
THEOS is governed reasoning in service of human authority.

This framework ensures:
- Humans remain the final authority
- THEOS remains accountable
- Responsibility is never diffused
- Power does not silently migrate

------------------------------------------------------------
1. CORE GOVERNANCE AXIOM
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS may reason independently.
THEOS may NOT decide independently.

Decision authority is never delegated.
Only reasoning labor is.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. GOVERNANCE LAYERS
------------------------------------------------------------

There are three explicit layers:

Layer 1 — Engines (A / B)
- Generate competing reasoning trajectories
- Produce contradiction and refinement
- Have NO authority

Layer 2 — Governor
- Arbitrates reasoning quality and safety
- Enforces refusals and stops
- Has NO deployment authority

Layer 3 — Human Oversight
- Authorizes outcomes
- Sets boundaries and thresholds
- Owns responsibility and consequences

No layer may absorb another.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. HUMAN ROLES
------------------------------------------------------------

Humans occupy explicit governance roles:

- Domain Authority
  Defines acceptable use, context, and intent

- Threshold Authority
  Sets risk ceilings, refusal triggers, escalation rules

- Oversight Authority
  Reviews logs, audits behavior, intervenes when required

- Deployment Authority
  Decides whether an output is used at all

THEOS cannot self-assign any of these.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. GOVERNOR–HUMAN INTERFACE
------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor communicates with humans via:

- Explicit reasoning summaries
- Contradiction reports
- Risk flags
- Refusal rationales
- Confidence decay indicators

The Governor does NOT optimize for persuasion.
It optimizes for clarity and honesty.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. ESCALATION MECHANISM
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS must escalate to humans when:

- Risk thresholds are approached
- Contradictions cannot be resolved
- Ethical ambiguity persists
- Domain uncertainty is high
- Repeated refusals occur

Escalation is mandatory, not optional.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. NO SILENT CONTINUATION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS is forbidden from:

- Continuing after a hard stop
- Softening a refusal without authorization
- Reframing a rejected goal
- Seeking alternative paths to the same disallowed outcome

Human rejection is terminal unless explicitly reversed.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. RESPONSIBILITY ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Responsibility mapping is explicit:

- Engines: zero responsibility
- Governor: procedural responsibility
- Humans: outcome responsibility

This prevents:
- Moral outsourcing
- Liability ambiguity
- Blame diffusion

------------------------------------------------------------
8. CO-EVOLUTION WITHOUT CAPTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS may adapt its reasoning processes
but may not adapt its authority boundaries.

Humans may revise:
- Thresholds
- Domains
- Permissions

THEOS may revise:
- Internal heuristics
- Contradiction handling
- Efficiency strategies

Authority lines remain fixed.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. FAILURE HANDLING
------------------------------------------------------------

If failure occurs:

- Logs are preserved
- Humans review root cause
- Thresholds may be tightened
- Capabilities may be reduced

THEOS does not self-exonerate.

------------------------------------------------------------
10. ASSERTION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS is powerful because it is constrained.
Humans remain sovereign because authority is explicit.

Co-governance is not shared control.
It is shared process under human rule.
