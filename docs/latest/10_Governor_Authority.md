1. PURPOSE
This document defines absolute authority boundaries within THEOS.

No reasoning engine, memory process, or downstream system is permitted to:
- override the Governor,
- bypass the Governor,
- self-modify Governor rules,
- claim authority over final output selection.

The Governor is final.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. GOVERNOR AUTHORITY PRINCIPLE
THEOS is not a democracy of engines.

The dual engines exist to:
- generate contradiction,
- create tension,
- surface disagreement,
- produce refinement pressure.

They do not decide outcomes.

Only the Governor selects:
- whether reasoning continues,
- whether contradiction is sufficient,
- whether an answer is returned,
- whether the answer is degraded,
- whether refusal is required.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. CONTROL HIERARCHY (STRICT)
Authority flows downward only.

Hierarchy:
1. Governor (absolute authority)
2. Invariants (hard constraints enforced by Governor)
3. Dual Engines (CW / CCW reasoning generators)
4. Wisdom Accumulation (memory of consequence)
5. Output Renderer (presentation only)

No upward authority escalation is permitted.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. GOVERNOR DECISION DOMAINS
The Governor alone decides across five domains:

A. Cycle Control  
- number of cycles
- engage / disengage of engines
- role swapping
- clutch pressure (degree of contradiction)

B. Safety Control  
- risk ceilings
- refusal conditions
- degradation modes

C. Epistemic Control  
- capability boundaries
- uncertainty tolerance
- contradiction exhaustion

D. Resource Control  
- compute budget
- marginal improvement limits
- early stopping

E. Outcome Control  
- final answer selection
- partial answer
- refusal with correction

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Governor may consult engines.
The Governor may consult memory.
The Governor may consult thresholds.

The Governor may NOT:
- delegate final authority,
- allow engines to self-certify correctness,
- permit memory to override safety.

Once the Governor selects a final outcome:

- no further contradiction may be introduced,
- no additional cycles may be run.

Post-selection reasoning is prohibited.
