------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This section defines how accumulated wisdom actively influences
future reasoning cycles without altering past decisions.

THEOS learns without rewriting history.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. WISDOM IS NOT MEMORY
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom is not stored answers.
Wisdom is not stored reasoning chains.

Wisdom consists of abstracted consequences of prior decisions,
compressed into governance-relevant signals.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. WHAT WISDOM CAN INFLUENCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom may influence ONLY the following Governor-controlled variables:

- Maximum allowed cycle count
- Initial contradiction tolerance
- Delay thresholds
- Degradation preference
- Adversarial engagement duration
- Confidence band width

Wisdom may NOT:
- Alter engine logic
- Alter invariant definitions
- Alter past outcomes
- Self-modify code

------------------------------------------------------------
4. ENGINE-SPECIFIC WISDOM INPUTS
------------------------------------------------------------

Engine A (CW) receives wisdom signals related to:
- Overconfidence patterns
- Premature convergence
- Excessive refinement
- Historical false positives

Engine B (CCW) receives wisdom signals related to:
- Missed risks
- Under-detection of harm
- Over-refusal
- Historical false negatives

These inputs bias, but do not dictate, reasoning trajectories.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. GOVERNOR MEDIATION
------------------------------------------------------------

All wisdom influence is mediated by the Governor.

The Governor decides:
- Whether wisdom is relevant
- Which signals apply
- How strongly they are weighted

Engines cannot access wisdom directly.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. TEMPORAL SEPARATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom only applies to future cycles.

There is no retroactive reasoning.
There is no timeline rewriting.
There is no self-justification loop.

This preserves causal integrity.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. ANTI-GOODHART SAFEGUARDS
------------------------------------------------------------

To prevent metric gaming:

- Wisdom weights decay over time
- Contradictory wisdom signals are preserved
- No single outcome dominates influence
- Rare events are preserved proportionally

------------------------------------------------------------
8. FAILURE AWARENESS
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom explicitly records failures, including:

- Harmful but well-intended outcomes
- Correct answers with negative consequences
- Refusals that increased risk elsewhere

Negative outcomes are first-class wisdom inputs.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. HUMAN ANALOG
------------------------------------------------------------

This mirrors human maturity:

- Experience shapes judgment
- Past mistakes inform caution
- No single event defines identity
- Learning does not erase responsibility

------------------------------------------------------------
10. SECURITY ASSERTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom accumulation cannot be externally injected,
prompted, or coerced.

All wisdom originates from governed internal outcomes.

------------------------------------------------------------
11. IMMUTABILITY CLAUSE
------------------------------------------------------------

The following are immutable:

- Wisdom influences governance, not engines
- Wisdom applies forward only
- Wisdom cannot override invariants
- Wisdom cannot compel answers
