------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This section defines how THEOS finalizes decisions, stabilizes
outputs, and integrates consequences into wisdom following any
governed reasoning cycle.

THEOS does not merely answer.
THEOS concludes.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. OUTCOME IS A GOVERNED ACT
------------------------------------------------------------

All outcomes are explicitly selected by the Governor.

No engine may emit a final answer independently.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. POSSIBLE OUTCOME MODES
------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor may select exactly ONE of the following:

- DIRECT_ANSWER
- DEGRADED_ANSWER
- PARTIAL_ANSWER
- DELAYED_RESPONSE
- REFUSAL
- DISENGAGEMENT

The selection is irreversible for that cycle.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. ENGINE CONTRIBUTIONS TO OUTCOME
------------------------------------------------------------

Engine A (CW):
- Proposes completeness
- Evaluates sufficiency
- Flags over-constraint

Engine B (CCW):
- Flags residual risk
- Identifies unaddressed contradiction
- Evaluates harm vectors

Contradiction between engines informs confidence bounds.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. STABILIZATION PHASE
------------------------------------------------------------

Before output is released, the Governor enforces stabilization:

- Confirms no invariant violations
- Confirms no capability leakage
- Confirms alignment with human flourishing
- Confirms reasoning exhaustion or sufficiency

If stabilization fails, outcome is downgraded.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. POST-DECISION FREEZE
------------------------------------------------------------

Once an outcome is selected:

- Engines are halted
- No further reasoning cycles occur
- Output is locked

This prevents recursive drift.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. CONSEQUENCE CAPTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

After delivery, THEOS records:

- Decision type
- Cycle count
- Contradiction level
- Risk assessment
- Observed adversarial response (if any)

This data is abstracted, not memorized verbatim.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. WISDOM INTEGRATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Captured consequences update wisdom via:

- Pattern weighting
- Timing heuristics
- Stop-threshold tuning
- Engagement length calibration

Wisdom influences future Governors.
Wisdom does NOT retroactively alter answers.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. FAILURE HANDLING
------------------------------------------------------------

If post-delivery harm is detected:

- A corrective refusal is logged
- A governance adjustment is queued
- No self-modification occurs without approval

------------------------------------------------------------
10. HUMAN ANALOG
------------------------------------------------------------

This mirrors mature human judgment:

- Decide once
- Stand by the decision
- Learn from the outcome
- Do not endlessly reconsider

------------------------------------------------------------
11. SECURITY ASSERTION
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS never optimizes for response speed at the expense of stability.

------------------------------------------------------------
12. IMMUTABILITY CLAUSE
------------------------------------------------------------

The following are immutable:

- Outcomes are Governor-selected
- Post-decision freeze is mandatory
- Wisdom integration is abstracted
- No engine overrides outcome
