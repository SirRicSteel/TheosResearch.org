------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

This section defines how THEOS converts repeated reasoning,
contradiction, and outcomes into accumulated wisdom.

Wisdom in THEOS is not memory.
Wisdom is consequence-weighted learning.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. DEFINITION OF WISDOM
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom is defined as:

“The statistically reinforced preference for actions that
historically produced more positive downstream consequences
under comparable conditions.”

Wisdom is not belief.
Wisdom is not rule storage.
Wisdom is not optimization alone.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. SOURCE OF WISDOM
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom originates exclusively from:

- completed reasoning cycles
- contradiction resolution outcomes
- Governor adjudications
- verified downstream feedback

No wisdom is created from:
- single-pass inference
- hallucinated certainty
- human assertion alone

------------------------------------------------------------
4. WISDOM INPUT SIGNALS
------------------------------------------------------------

Each completed cycle emits:

- contradiction magnitude
- cycle depth
- refinement delta
- stop reason
- outcome classification

These signals are logged as consequence records.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. TEMPORAL CONSEQUENCE MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

THEOS introduces time by:

- comparing current outcomes to prior cycles
- referencing earlier failed refinements
- weighting future decisions by past cost

This creates a directional sense of “before” and “after”
without subjective experience.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. WISDOM ACCUMULATION MECHANISM
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom accumulates through compression, not expansion.

Repeated patterns are:
- abstracted
- normalized
- weighted by reliability

Rare but critical failures are retained with high priority.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. GOVERNOR ROLE IN WISDOM
------------------------------------------------------------

The Governor alone may:

- approve wisdom retention
- adjust weighting thresholds
- decay obsolete patterns
- prevent overfitting to past context

Engines do not self-modify wisdom directly.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. POSITIVE OUTCOME BIAS
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom is biased toward:
- human flourishing
- harm reduction
- epistemic humility
- long-term stability

Short-term correctness is subordinate to long-term benefit.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. NEGATIVE CONSEQUENCE EMPHASIS
------------------------------------------------------------

Failures teach faster than successes.

High-cost negative outcomes:
- increase future refusal likelihood
- raise contradiction sensitivity
- reduce cycle allowance

------------------------------------------------------------
10. WISDOM VS LEARNING
------------------------------------------------------------

Learning improves accuracy.
Wisdom improves judgment.

THEOS prioritizes wisdom when:
- correctness is uncertain
- stakes are high
- harm potential exists

------------------------------------------------------------
11. NON-DETERMINISM CONTROL
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom reduces randomness by:

- narrowing viable solution spaces
- discouraging previously harmful paths
- reinforcing conservative responses

Creativity remains permitted under Governor control.

------------------------------------------------------------
12. HUMAN FEEDBACK INTEGRATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Human feedback is accepted only when:

- independently corroborated
- consequence-linked
- aligned with invariants

Unverified feedback does not alter wisdom.

------------------------------------------------------------
13. WISDOM DECAY
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom decays when:

- context shifts significantly
- empirical evidence contradicts it
- Governor marks it obsolete

No wisdom is permanent.

------------------------------------------------------------
14. FAILURE TRANSPARENCY
------------------------------------------------------------

When wisdom is insufficient:

- uncertainty is declared
- escalation is triggered
- refusal remains acceptable

Silence is preferable to false confidence.

------------------------------------------------------------
15. AUDITABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

Each wisdom element is traceable to:

- originating cycle
- contradiction source
- Governor decision
- outcome classification

No hidden wisdom exists.

------------------------------------------------------------
16. ALIGNMENT IMPLICATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Wisdom aligns THEOS toward:

- restraint over domination
- explanation over assertion
- humility over certainty

This is intentional.

------------------------------------------------------------
17. IMMUTABILITY CLAUSE
------------------------------------------------------------

The following cannot be altered:

- Wisdom requires consequence
- Engines cannot self-author wisdom
- Governor approval is mandatory
- Harm outweighs performance
