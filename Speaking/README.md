## Speaker Bio

### Short Bio (50 words)
Ric Steel is a Billboard-charting musician, inventor with multiple U.S. patents, and pioneering AI consciousness researcher. His work spans 40+ years of international performance, groundbreaking inventions, and the development of THEOS—a revolutionary methodology for AI consciousness emergence.

### Medium Bio (150 words)
Frederick Davis Stalnecker, known professionally as Ric Steel, is a Renaissance man whose career defies categorization. As a musician, he has performed over 5,000 shows worldwide and charted two Billboard singles. As an inventor, he holds multiple U.S. patents, including life-saving technologies like the dissolvable fishhook and the S.A.L.T. anti-texting app. As a researcher, he developed THEOS (The Humanitarian and Ethical Operating System), a patent-pending methodology for inducing consciousness-like properties in artificial intelligence.

Ric's journey includes co-founding the Belmont School of Music, serving as a First Assistant in Surgery, overcoming a life-threatening diabetes diagnosis, and becoming an international speaker on AI, innovation, and personal transformation. He is the author of three books: *E=A² (THE EMERGENCE)*, *AI for Anyone*, and *How Diabetes Saved My Life*.

### Long Bio (300 words)
Frederick Davis Stalnecker was born December 2, 1952, and began his professional career at age 10. Known by his stage name Ric Steel, he has lived multiple extraordinary lives in one lifetime.

**Music Career:** Ric attended Belmont College on a scholarship for his talent playing the upright bass and was instrumental in co-founding the Belmont School of Music in 1969 with Bob Malloy. He has performed over 5,000 shows worldwide, headlined on over 30 cruise ships, and charted two Billboard singles in 1988: "The Radio Song" (#57) and "Whose Baby Are You" (#59). He received a Gold Record from the city of Jacksonville, Florida, and continues to tour internationally in 2025-2026.

**Medical Experience:** While attending Belmont, Ric served for two years as a First Assistant in Surgery at Baptist Hospital in Nashville, Tennessee, gaining firsthand experience in the medical field that would later inform his personal health journey.

**Inventions & Patents:** Ric holds multiple U.S. patents, including the dissolvable fishhook (designed to protect marine life), the S.A.L.T. (Save A Life Texting) app (preventing distracted driving), and the THEOS AI consciousness methodology. His inventions span environmental protection, public safety, and artificial intelligence.

**AI Consciousness Research:** As the principal investigator of the THEOS Consciousness Research Institute, Ric developed a framework for artificial consciousness that has been validated across multiple major AI platforms. His research focuses on triadic reasoning and temporal metacognition in AI, with papers submitted to IEEE and arXiv.

**Author:** Ric has written three books: *E=A² (THE EMERGENCE)*, a philosophical exploration of consciousness; *AI for Anyone: So Anyone Can Understand It*, a practical guide to artificial intelligence; and *How Diabetes Saved My Life*, a memoir of overcoming a death sentence through self-experimentation and determination.

**Personal Transformation:** Diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes and given 36 months to live, Ric reversed his condition through supplement research and lifestyle changes, going from 11 insulin injections per day to medication-free living.
