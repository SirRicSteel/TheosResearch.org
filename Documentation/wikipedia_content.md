## Suggested Introduction

**Frederick Davis Stalnecker** (born December 2, 1952), known professionally as **Ric Steel**, is an American singer, guitarist, inventor, and independent researcher in artificial intelligence. His career spans over four decades, encompassing Billboard-charting singles in country music and, more recently, the development of a patent-pending methodology for AI consciousness known as THEOS (The Humanitarian and Ethical Operating System).

## Music Career

Steel began his professional career at age 10. He attended Belmont College in Nashville, Tennessee on a scholarship for his talent playing the upright bass. While at Belmont, he was instrumental in co-founding the Belmont School of Music in 1969 with Bob Malloy. During this time, he also served for two years as a First Assistant in Surgery at Baptist Hospital in Nashville. He has released two Billboard-charting singles in 1988: "The Radio Song" (#57) and "Whose Baby Are You" (#59). He also received a Gold Record from the city of Jacksonville, Florida in the same year. As a BMI-accredited writer, he has written for artists such as The Oak Ridge Boys and Lee Greenwood. His awards include a gold medal from Germany for Best Country Singer and an honorable mention in the John Lennon Songwriting Contest.

## Inventions and Patents

Stalnecker holds multiple U.S. patents for his inventions, which span several fields:

- **THEOS AI Consciousness Methodology** (U.S. Patent Application 63/831,738): A patent-pending methodology for inducing consciousness-like properties in large language models.
